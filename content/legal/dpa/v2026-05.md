<p><strong>Between:</strong> Flinker GmbH, Zittelstra&szlig;e 7, 80796 Munich, Germany,
Amtsgericht M&uuml;nchen HRB 254870 (hereinafter &ldquo;Processor&rdquo;)<br>
<strong>and:</strong> the customer identified in the Main Contract (hereinafter &ldquo;Controller&rdquo;)</p>

<p>This DPA is incorporated into and forms part of the Main Contract between the Parties
and becomes effective upon execution of the Main Contract.</p>


<h2 id="s1">&sect; 1 Subject Matter and Term</h2>
<p><strong>1.1</strong> This DPA governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf
of the Controller pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR.</p>
<p><strong>1.2</strong> The subject matter is the operation of Flinker&rsquo;s Microsoft 365-native SaaS
applications (IFC Viewer for SharePoint, Teams, Excel, Power BI; SharePoint CDE; Copilot for IFC;
SharePoint Protect) used by the Controller under the Main Contract.</p>
<p><strong>1.3</strong> This DPA runs for the duration of the Main Contract and terminates automatically
upon its termination, subject to continuing obligations under &sect; 13.</p>


<h2 id="s2">&sect; 2 Nature and Purpose of Processing</h2>
<p><strong>2.1 Nature:</strong> Collection, storage, use, and deletion of personal data to the extent
strictly necessary for the provision of the contracted services.</p>
<p><strong>2.2 Purpose:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Authentication and authorisation of users against the Controller&rsquo;s Microsoft 365 tenant</li>
  <li>Operation and provision of Flinker applications</li>
  <li>Support and consulting services upon request of the Controller</li>
  <li>Anonymised usage analytics for product improvement</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>2.3 Architecture note:</strong> Flinker applications run as SPFx web parts entirely within
the Controller&rsquo;s Microsoft 365 tenant. IFC files, BIM models, and project data never leave the
Controller&rsquo;s tenant at any time. Viewer source code is delivered as static assets via Azure CDN;
no content data is transferred in this process.</p>
<p><strong>Exception &mdash; Copilot for IFC:</strong> IFC files are processed exclusively in the
user&rsquo;s browser. Only chat text (user inputs and AI responses) is transmitted to Microsoft Azure AI
(Azure OpenAI Service). No IFC file content leaves the browser.</p>


<h2 id="s3">&sect; 3 Categories of Personal Data and Data Subjects</h2>
<p><strong>Microsoft 365 Tenant ID:</strong> Required for authentication and licence verification. Does
not directly identify a natural person but enables attribution to the Controller&rsquo;s tenant.</p>
<p><strong>User email address (optional):</strong> Collected only in connection with support requests or
where explicitly provided during login. Not collected by default.</p>
<p><strong>Anonymised usage metrics:</strong> Aggregated, non-personal data on usage intensity. No
inference to natural persons possible.</p>
<p><strong>Chat inputs &mdash; Copilot for IFC only:</strong> Text-based user inputs processed via
Microsoft Azure AI. No IFC file content.</p>
<p><strong>Data subjects:</strong> IT administrators and tenant administrators of the Controller;
end users (employees) to the extent they use Flinker applications and engage optional features
involving personal data.</p>


<h2 id="s4">&sect; 4 Obligations of the Processor</h2>
<p><strong>4.1</strong> Processing only on documented Controller instructions (&sect; 6), unless
required by applicable law. In such cases the Processor informs the Controller prior to processing,
unless prohibited by law.</p>
<p><strong>4.2</strong> All persons authorised to process personal data are subject to appropriate
confidentiality obligations by contract or by operation of law.</p>
<p><strong>4.3</strong> The Processor implements the technical and organisational measures described
in &sect; 8.</p>
<p><strong>4.4</strong> The Processor assists the Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests
and obligations under Art. 32&ndash;36 GDPR.</p>
<p><strong>4.5</strong> Data protection contact: <a href="mailto:privacy@flinker.app">privacy@flinker.app</a></p>


<h2 id="s5">&sect; 5 Obligations of the Controller</h2>
<p><strong>5.1</strong> The Controller is solely responsible for the lawfulness of processing within
its sphere of responsibility.</p>
<p><strong>5.2</strong> The Controller issues instructions in writing or documented electronic format.</p>
<p><strong>5.3</strong> The Controller warrants entitlement to transfer personal data to the Processor.</p>
<p><strong>5.4</strong> The Controller notifies the Processor without undue delay upon identifying
errors or irregularities in the Processor&rsquo;s activities.</p>


<h2 id="s6">&sect; 6 Instructions</h2>
<p><strong>6.1</strong> The Processor processes personal data only on documented instructions.
The Main Contract and any Statements of Work constitute the initial instructions.</p>
<p><strong>6.2</strong> Instructions by email to:
<a href="mailto:privacy@flinker.app">privacy@flinker.app</a></p>
<p><strong>6.3</strong> If the Processor considers an instruction unlawful, it notifies the Controller
without undue delay and may suspend execution.</p>


<h2 id="s7">&sect; 7 Confidentiality</h2>
<p>The Processor binds all employees and service providers with access to the Controller&rsquo;s
personal data to appropriate confidentiality obligations. These obligations survive termination
of the relevant employment or service relationship.</p>


<h2 id="s8">&sect; 8 Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs)</h2>
<p>Full TOM documentation: <a href="https://flinker.app/tom/">flinker.app/tom</a></p>

<p><strong>Pseudonymisation and encryption</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Pseudonymisation where technically feasible and proportionate</li>
  <li>Encryption of all systems storing or processing personal data</li>
  <li>HTTPS / TLS 1.2 or higher for all data transmission</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Confidentiality and integrity</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Exclusive use of Microsoft Azure EU infrastructure (Germany, Ireland)</li>
  <li>Production access restricted to authorised Germany-based staff</li>
  <li>Azure AD token-based authentication; no proprietary auth provider</li>
  <li>SPFx web parts execute JavaScript in the user&rsquo;s browser only</li>
  <li>Azure Authentication Log for access control and monitoring</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Availability and resilience</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Regular data backups provided by Microsoft Azure</li>
  <li>Monitoring via Azure Log Analytics</li>
  <li>Option for complete self-hosting within the customer&rsquo;s Azure tenant</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Review and evaluation</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Internal control system for regular TOM review and updating</li>
  <li>Code reviews and integration testing incorporating security requirements</li>
  <li>Agile change management with automated tests before deployment</li>
</ul>

Changes notified at least 30 days in advance. The Controller may object on substantiated data
protection grounds within that period.</p>

<h2 id="s10">&sect; 10 Assistance with Data Subject Rights</h2>
<p>The Processor assists the Controller in responding to data subject requests under Art. 15&ndash;22 GDPR
(access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection).
Erasure requests are fulfilled at no additional charge.</p>


<h2 id="s11">&sect; 11 Personal Data Breach Notification</h2>
<p><strong>11.1</strong> Notification within <strong>36 hours</strong> of becoming aware, by email to the
Controller&rsquo;s designated contact.</p>
<p><strong>11.2</strong> Notification includes: nature of the breach; categories and estimated number of
data subjects affected; likely consequences; remedial measures taken or planned.</p>
<p><strong>11.3</strong> As the Processor relies entirely on Microsoft Azure infrastructure, a security
incident can only arise through Microsoft Azure. The Processor notifies the Controller immediately
upon becoming aware.</p>


<h2 id="s12">&sect; 12 Audit Rights and Evidence of Compliance</h2>
<p><strong>12.1</strong> The Processor makes available all information necessary to demonstrate
Art. 28 GDPR compliance.</p>
<p><strong>12.2</strong> Audits with at least 14 days&rsquo; written notice, during business hours, limited
to data protection-relevant processing aspects. Costs borne by the Controller.</p>
<p><strong>12.3</strong> Microsoft infrastructure audits are referred directly to Microsoft.</p>


<h2 id="s13">&sect; 13 Deletion and Return of Data upon Termination</h2>
<p><strong>13.1</strong> Upon termination, the Processor deletes all Controller personal data without
undue delay, unless a statutory retention obligation applies.</p>
<p><strong>13.2</strong> IFC files and project data remain exclusively within the Controller&rsquo;s tenant;
their deletion is the Controller&rsquo;s responsibility. The Processor deletes only Processor-side data:
Tenant ID, optional email address, usage metrics.</p>
<p><strong>13.3</strong> The Processor provides written deletion confirmation upon request.</p>


<h2 id="s14">&sect; 14 Transfers to Third Countries</h2>
<p><strong>14.1</strong> Processing on EU servers (Germany and Ireland).</p>
<p><strong>14.2</strong> Microsoft third-country transfers are covered by the EU&ndash;U.S. Data Privacy
Framework (Commission adequacy decision, 10 July 2023) and Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC)
pursuant to Implementing Decision 2021/914/EU.</p>
<p><strong>14.3</strong> No other third-country transfers by the Processor.</p>


<h2 id="s15">&sect; 15 Final Provisions</h2>
<p><strong>15.1</strong> This DPA forms part of the Main Contract. In the event of conflict, this DPA
prevails on data protection matters.</p>
<p><strong>15.2</strong> Amendments require written form or documented electronic agreement.</p>
<p><strong>15.3</strong> Governed by the laws of the Federal Republic of Germany.
Exclusive jurisdiction: Munich.</p>
<p><strong>15.4</strong> Severability: invalidity of any provision does not affect remaining provisions.</p>
<p><strong>15.5</strong> Effective upon execution of the Main Contract. Enterprise customers may download
the PDF version for countersignature.</p>
